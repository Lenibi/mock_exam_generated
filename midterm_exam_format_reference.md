Question 34		4 / 5 points
Explain the difference between local (object) space, world space, and camera (view) space, and why transformations between these spaces are necessary.

Answer:	These spaces matter because of the Geometry stages. In the main geometry stage of the rendering pipeline, an object starts off with local coordinates. The object is then projected to the world space, transforming the coordinates from it's own local coordinates to relative to the scene. This is crucial because object space coordinates are relative to itself, while in world space it's relative to the entire scene and all the other objects from the origin. The origin in object is itself, but in world space the origin is in the entire scene. Then, this world space is projected into the camera view space. Transformations between these spaces are necessary because the coordinates change as they are relative to something else. An object's scale, position, coordinates etc. are not the same as relative to the entire scene, therefore a transformation must happen to change the values.	Incorrect Response(Local (object) space defines vertex positions relative to an individual object’s own coordinate system. World space represents the global coordinate system in which all objects in the scene are positioned relative to each other. Camera (view) space defines geometry relative to the camera’s position and orientation. Transformations are necessary so that objects can be correctly positioned in the scene and then rendered from the camera’s point of view, ensuring that all geometry is expressed in a common reference frame for projection and visibility calculations.)

Question 39		5 / 5 points
How does view-dependent LoD differ from traditional LoD, and what advantage does it provide?

Answer:	In traditional LOD, objects farther away from the camera (a greater ecludian distance) have lower LOD because they are seen as much smaller when farther away so they require less detail. View dependent LOD is based on rotation and orientation, which way the object is facing. Polygons that are facing opposite direction from the Camera have lower level of detail because they are not seen as much from the Camera POV. The advantages this provides is it allows objects to have different levels of LOD within them (one polygon could be lower LOD because its facing away from the camera, while one facing towards the camera has higher LOD). This is a big advantage for closer objects, because in traditional only far objects have lower LOD, but any close objects have high LOD no matter which way it's polygons are facing. Hidden objects inside of a room are also lower LOD because the camera does not see them as much or they are hidden from the camera therefore not seen.	Incorrect Response(Unlike traditional LoD, which assigns a single resolution level to an entire object based primarily on distance, view-dependent LoD adjusts detail dynamically across different regions of the same object. Portions closer to the camera or near silhouette edges are rendered at higher resolution, while distant or interior regions are simplified. This provides better granularity and allocates polygons where they contribute most to perceived visual quality.)
